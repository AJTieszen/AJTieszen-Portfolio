CSU Pueblo Robotics Team (2022)

Team Members:

  • Josh Cutler
  • Andrew Tieszen

Project Description:

Every year, the Colorado Space Grant Consortium hosts their Robotics Challenge. The stated goal of the challenge is to develop a rover under a certain weight limit, which is capable of autonomously navigating various obstacle courses set up at Great Sand Dunes National Park in Colorado.

For the 2022 season, the Team AJ, named for the first initials of the participants, constructed a rover utilizing 3D printed tank tracks, a first for the competition. The development process included plenty of incremental testing, as shown to the rightbelow, before competing in the actual competition. Although the design still had some hurdles to overcome, the team won an award on competition day, 16 April 2022, for an "outstanding demonstration of creative locomotion.
